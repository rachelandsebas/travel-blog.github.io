## Christmas

We arrived in Ecuador early on December 24th, the day Christmas is celebrated.

![Enjoying shrimp soup after arriving in Ecuador](images/Arrival.jpeg)

That evening, we went to Sebastián’s Aunt Julia’s house to celebrate Christmas. We enjoyed seeing the aunts, uncles, and cousins and hearing updates on how everyone is doing. We brought fireworks and Roman Candles, which we set off with some of the kids there.

Christmas is also Sebastián’s Aunt Ana’s birthday. This year, her kids surprised her with a mariachi band! We snuck the band in when she was in the kitchen, and then when she came out they started singing for her. For the next few hours they led the party in music and dance.

![The mariachi band](images/Christmas_6.mp4)

One evening, Sebastián’s Uncle Leonardo, who is a farmer, brought over a whole leg of pork, which he had roasted himself.

It smelled incredible. Uncle Leonardo came in a taxi, and the poor taxi driver had to spend 40 minutes with the mouth-watering food there. It was only fair then that they gave him a plate too.

That one leg of pork served over twenty people, and was delicious!!
